challenge: |
  The organisation's content had grown organically over many years, with no central strategy, inconsistent messaging, and no clear ownership. Teams were creating content in silos, duplicating effort, and struggling to reach the people who needed their services most.

  The result was a website full of outdated, overlapping content that didn't serve users or the organisation's goals.
approach: |
  We started with a comprehensive content audit and stakeholder interviews across six departments. User research — including interviews and usability testing — revealed critical gaps between what the organisation was saying and what people needed to hear.

  From there, we developed a content strategy framework covering governance, workflows, messaging priorities, and a phased roadmap for implementation.
impact: |
  The new strategy gave the organisation a shared language for talking about content and clear ownership for the first time. Within six months, they reduced their page count by 40% while increasing engagement on key service pages.

  The governance framework is still in use today, guiding decisions about what to create, maintain, and retire.
