challenge: |
  The website had grown to over 3,000 pages across events, exhibitions, education, and membership. Users were struggling to find what they needed, with search and navigation designed around the organisation's internal structure rather than user needs.

  Analytics showed high bounce rates on key landing pages and frequent dead-end journeys.
approach: |
  We ran card sorting exercises with 40 participants and tree testing with a further 60 to understand how audiences naturally group and look for content. Stakeholder workshops mapped business priorities against user needs.

  The new IA was designed iteratively, tested at each stage, and validated against real user tasks before handover to the development team.
impact: |
  Task completion rates improved by 35% in post-launch testing. The simplified top-level navigation reduced the average number of clicks to key content from 4.2 to 2.1.

  The IA framework now serves as the foundation for all new content, with clear rules for where things live and how they connect.
