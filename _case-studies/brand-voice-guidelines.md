challenge: |
  Content was being produced by over 20 people across multiple teams, each with their own interpretation of the brand. The result was communications that ranged from overly clinical to inappropriately casual, with no consistent personality.

  Staff reported feeling unsure about how to write, often defaulting to corporate jargon to play it safe.
approach: |
  We audited existing content across channels — website, social, email, print — and mapped the tonal range. Workshops with staff and service users explored how the organisation wanted to sound versus how it was actually perceived.

  The voice framework was co-designed with the content team, ensuring practical buy-in from the people who would use it every day.
impact: |
  The guidelines were adopted across all teams within three months. Content review time dropped significantly as writers had clear, practical reference points for every piece they created.

  Staff confidence in writing increased measurably, and the organisation's communications developed a distinctive, consistent personality for the first time.
